## Synopsis

### Synopsis

Three friends performing a memorial service by the river transition to a time different from reality upon the appearance of a "yokai," an intermediary between humans and nature. Traveling through spacetime bizarrely twisted around the yokai, the friends fall behind one by one, and the last one to reach a Paleolithic cave entrusts their deepest wound to nature.

### Plot

- Intro (Subtitles):
  - Two people (A, B) attempted suicide by jumping off a bridge over the Han River. B jumped and died, but A ran away and tried to go to another world through a tunnel. However, a construction site blocked the tunnel. B was dragged into the river by a "yokai who crossed an uncrossable bridge" at a "place where the infinitely distant future and the infinitely distant past are connected."
- Main:
  - By a river in lush nature, three people (B, C, D) perform a memorial service facing the river, and A is filming them. The three people (B, C, D), meeting after a long time, chat and catch up in front of the memorial table. Then, a green yokai that crossed the river appears from the opposite side. The three people (B, C, D) throw stones at the yokai. A throws a rock at the yokai with all their might, but the rock misses, orbits the earth, and comes back. The rock shatters the screen, revealing an inner screen behind it. The three people (B, C, D) on that screen are looking at the river.
  - A's corpse floats down from upstream and gets caught on a stone pagoda. When the yokai on the opposite side of the river reels in a fishing line, the corpse moves upstream, exiting the screen to the right while simultaneously entering from the left. The yokai carrying this screen crosses the river and climbs the mountain on the opposite side.
  - As the camera pans 90 degrees, the mountains on the left and right across the river are shown, and an illusion of a bridge connecting them appears. This screen is distorted/shrunk and overlaps with a smaller screen inside a frame, connecting the mountain ridges in the two frames. The original frame disappears, and when the camera pans 90 degrees again, the three people (B, C, D) from the past who came to play by the river are shown in the small frame. They discover the memorial table placed by the river and wonder why it is there. The yokai reels in the fishing line and catches a fish. Then it crosses the river, approaches the three people (B, C, D), and performs a memorial service.
  - The three people (B, C, D) feel curious about the yokai and ask various questions. When B asks, "How do you eat a fish?", the yokai cuts off the fish's head. (It doesn't appear in the film, but the crew cooked and ate the fish.) The frame, which slowly grew at an imperceptible speed, finally occupies the entire screen. The same video as the full screen is playing inside the funeral portrait frame placed on the memorial table.
  - The yokai guides the three people (B, C, D) to the mountain. At a crossroad, the yokai briefly goes down the lower path and returns carrying an empty screen. C takes this screen, examines it closely, and falls behind. D also gets exhausted and drops out. B and the yokai continue climbing the mountain and reach the summit. The yokai falls off a cliff and orbits the earth (infinite fall). The earth transforms into a torus shape.
  - On the laterally inverted mountain summit, A, who has traveled full circle through time (assuming the time axis is a straight line and treating it as an infinitely large circle), appears and meets B for the first time. The conversation between the two is misaligned yet interlocking, creating the line "Where did the disappeared bridge go?"
  - This screen transforms into a wormhole shape, and the yokai and B, who walked from off-screen, pass through the wormhole. They emerge through a cave (an actual site where Paleolithic artifacts were found) on the opposite side of the wormhole. In front of the cave is a river, and an apartment complex can be seen across it.
  - The yokai peels off the grass covering its face from its chin to its nose, revealing that the yokai was A. The yokai (A) licks the scar on B's wrist. As it licks for a long time, the scar fades away.
  - The yokai also disappears. A drop of water falls on B's arm. The red lights of the bridge across the river are covered in green.

### Screening/Exhibition

- The screening/exhibition at SNU Design Week took place in two locations: a screening room and an exhibition room.
  - In the screening room, the film could be viewed in a theater-like environment.
  - In the exhibition room, the film could be viewed in a space surrounded by white walls on three sides.
    - The film was projected on the front wall, and beneath the movie screen, a camera capable of streaming real-time video to a PC was installed facing the viewers. On the side walls, real-time CG-processed videos of the viewers captured by the camera were played.
    - The video from '1 minute ago' was played on the left wall.
    - A video processed with a 'wormhole effect' was played on the right wall.

## Commentary

- This work is a short film centered on the theme of a desire to heal trauma through nonlinear time existing within nature and CG.
- In expressing the psychologically perceived structure of time, spatial geometry/topological ideas are applied.
  - The idea that past events exist as certain objects in the present often appears in the history of religion and philosophy. This work treats video as 'materialized memory' as an object within a higher-level video, distorting it through various transformations, and proposes the establishment of spatial or topological relationships between video objects as a cinematic method of expressing stories about time, karma, and healing.
    - For example, a shot where an object moves as if the left and right sides of the screen are connected, or the earth transforming into a torus shape, represent topological variations of video objects. This implies that the space reflected in the video is 'a space where karma cannot converge into a single point.'
- In filming, a 'camera looking at the past' was implemented by adhering to spatial rules regarding characters' movements.
  - Movement on the horizontal axis: Means movement within the same time zone.
  - Movement on the depth axis: Means traveling further back into the past as it moves away from the camera.
  - Upward movement on the vertical axis: Functions as a metaphor for things difficult to overcome in reality.

## Technology

- For film editing, After Effects and Unity were used to apply CG effects to the footage shot by the cinematographer.
  - ([Unity project source code used for the scene where the video object transforms from a rectangle to a wormhole](https://github.com/random-entity/o.mod.video-wormhole))
  - ([Unity project source code used for the scene where the earth transforms from a sphere to a torus](https://github.com/random-entity/o.mod.sphere-to-torus))
  - ([Unity project source code used for the scene where the rock orbiting the earth shatters the screen](https://github.com/random-entity/o.mod.mirror-shatter))
- For the real-time CG rendering on both walls of the exhibition room screening, TouchDesigner was used for the delay effect on the left wall, and Unity was used for the wormhole effect on the right wall.
